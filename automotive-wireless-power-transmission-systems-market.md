<p><ul><li>Electromagnetic Induction</li><li>Magnetic Resonance</li><li>Magneto-Dynamic Coupling</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive wireless power transmission systems utilize different technologies to transfer electrical energy wirelessly within a vehicle or to charge its batteries. The three main market types include electromagnetic induction, magnetic resonance, and magneto-dynamic coupling. Electromagnetic induction operates by creating a magnetic field that induces a current in a receiving coil. Magnetic resonance technology involves resonant circuits that transfer power across larger distances. Magneto-dynamic coupling works by transmitting power through a rotating magnetic field generated by a rotating permanent magnet and a stator assembly. These technologies enable efficient wireless charging and power transfer in automotive applications.</p></p>

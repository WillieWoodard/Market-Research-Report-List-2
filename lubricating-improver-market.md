<p><p>A lubricating improver, also known as a lubricant additive, is a substance added to lubricants to enhance their performance and improve their overall lubrication properties. These additives offer improved lubrication performance and exhibit fewer toxic effects compared to traditional petroleum-based additives.</p><p>Another trend in the lubricating improver market is the development of multifunctional additives. Manufacturers are increasingly focusing on developing additives that offer multiple benefits, such as reducing friction, enhancing wear protection, providing detergency, and improving viscosity index stability. These multifunctional additives help in optimizing lubricant performance and reducing the need for multiple additives.</p><p>Furthermore, the lubricating improver market is experiencing technological advancements, such as the development of nanolubricant additives. <p><p>The lubricating improver market can be classified into two types: acidic lubricating improver and non-acidic lubricating improver. Acidic lubricating improvers contain acid components that enhance the lubricating properties of oils by reducing friction and wear. They are often used in heavy-duty applications. On the other hand, non-acidic lubricating improvers do not contain acid components but still offer improved lubrication and protection against friction and wear. They are commonly used in automotive and industrial applications, providing long-lasting performance and engine protection.</p></p>

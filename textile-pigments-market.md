<p><p>Textile pigments are colorants used to impart various hues to fabrics. Disperse pigments are widely used to dye synthetic fibers like polyester, while reactive pigments react chemically with fabrics to form a covalent bond. Sulfur pigments are mainly used for dyeing cellulose-based fibers with deep colors. VAT pigments are used for dyeing cotton with excellent colorfastness, while acid pigments have good solubility in water and are commonly used for wool and silk. Lastly, there are various other pigments that cater to specific textile materials and dyeing processes, offering a wide range of color options.</p></p>

<p><p>Acousto-Optic Multi-Channel Modulators (AOMC) are devices commonly used in optic communication systems. They can be categorized into two market types: Free-Space Acousto-Optic Multi-Channel Modulators (AOMC) and Fiber-Coupled Acousto-Optic Multi-Channel Modulators (AOMC). </p><p>Free-space AOMCs operate in open space, where an acoustic wave interacts with an optic beam to create modulation. They are suitable for applications that require high-speed modulation and versatility. </p><p>On the other hand, fiber-coupled AOMCs are designed to be integrated with optical fibers. This allows for efficient coupling between the acoustic wave and the optic beam, making them suitable for fiber-optic communication systems, especially in long-distance scenarios.</p></p>

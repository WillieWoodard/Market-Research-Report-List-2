<p><p>The phenolic resin for buildings market mainly consists of two types of resins: thermoplastic phenolic resin and thermosetting phenolic resin. Thermoplastic phenolic resin is a type of resin that can be melted and re-molded multiple times without losing its properties. It is used in various building applications such as insulation materials and adhesives. On the other hand, thermosetting phenolic resin is a type of resin that, once cured, cannot be melted or re-molded. It is commonly used in applications that require high heat resistance, such as coatings and fire-resistant materials in buildings.</p></p>

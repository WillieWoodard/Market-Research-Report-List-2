<p><p>Reverse osmosis (RO) film is a crucial component in reverse osmosis systems used for water purification. There are two main types of RO films: cellulose-based and thin film composite (TFC) membranes. Cellulose-based membranes are typically made from plant fibrous materials and offer good performance at low pressures. On the other hand, TFC membranes are composed of multiple layers of polymer films, providing better salt rejection and higher water production rate. Both types of RO films contribute to effective water purification through the process of reverse osmosis.</p></p>

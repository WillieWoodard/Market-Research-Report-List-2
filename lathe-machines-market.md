<p><p>Lathe machines are classified into two main market types based on their orientation: vertical lathe and horizontal lathe. Vertical lathe machines have a vertical spindle axis and the workpiece is held vertically. They are well-suited for machining heavy and large workpieces with effective chip disposal. On the other hand, horizontal lathe machines have a horizontal spindle axis and the workpiece is held horizontally. They are commonly used for machining smaller and lighter workpieces with versatility for various cutting operations. The choice between vertical and horizontal lathe depends on the specific machining requirements and preferences of the user.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Clark Electrode Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Liquid-phase Electrode</li><li>Gas-phase Electrode</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Clark Electrode market consists of two types, namely the liquid-phase electrode and the gas-phase electrode. The liquid-phase electrode is designed to measure the oxygen concentration in an aqueous medium, such as blood samples. It consists of a platinum cathode and a silver/silver chloride anode immersed in a potassium chloride electrolyte solution. On the other hand, the gas-phase electrode is used to measure the oxygen concentration in a gaseous environment. It is composed of a platinum cathode, a lead anode, and a PTFE membrane that allows gas diffusion for accurate measurements.</p></p>

<p><p>Wheat flour market offers various types of flour, each designed for specific culinary purposes. Self-raising flour has added raising agents, ideal for making baked goods rise. Bread flour, high in gluten, creates a chewy texture in bread. Biscuit flour is low-gluten, resulting in a tender and flaky texture. Cake flour is finely ground, yielding a light and delicate crumb in cakes. Whole meal flour retains the bran and germ, providing a nutritious option. Resultant flour combines different flours for various uses. Semolina is coarse wheat flour suitable for pasta and dessert making.</p></p>

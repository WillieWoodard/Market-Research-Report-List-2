<p><p>An oil boiler is a heating device that uses oil as a fuel source to heat water or generate steam. It is commonly used in residential, commercial, and industrial settings for space heating and hot water production. The oil is burned in a combustion chamber and the heat generated is transferred to the water, which is then circulated throughout the building via pipes and radiators or underfloor heating systems.</p><p>The oil boiler market has been experiencing steady growth over the years, fueled by the increasing demand for efficient and reliable heating systems. Factors such as the rising adoption of oil-fired boilers in regions with limited access to natural gas, the need for space heating in colder climates, and the versatility of oil boilers in providing both heat and hot water have contributed to the market growth.</p><p>Additionally, technological advancements have played a significant role in boosting the market.

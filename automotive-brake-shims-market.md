<p><p>Automotive brake shims are thin pieces of material, usually made of rubber or adhesive-backed metal, which are placed between the brake pad and the brake caliper. Their purpose is to reduce noise and vibration caused by the contact between the two surfaces. These shims act as a dampening mechanism, absorbing the impact and reducing brake squeal.</p><p>The current outlook of the automotive brake shims market is positive. As the automotive industry continues to grow, the demand for quieter and more comfortable vehicles is increasing, leading to a higher demand for brake shims. Additionally, the increasing consumer awareness regarding safety and noise reduction in vehicles is also driving the market growth.</p><p>The future of the automotive brake shims market looks promising. With the advancements in technology, manufacturers are focusing on developing advanced brake shims that offer better noise reduction and improved performance. Additionally, the demand for eco-friendly brake shims that are free from hazardous substances is also increasing. Thermosetting Material refers to shims made of a material that hardens and remains rigid after being heated.

<p><p>The Fiberglass Insulation market consists of three main types: Continuous Fiber, Fixed Length Fiber, and Glass Wool. Continuous Fiber refers to insulation made from a long, unbroken strand of fiberglass, providing a seamless and efficient insulation solution. Fixed Length Fiber, on the other hand, is made from fiberglass strands of predetermined lengths, offering versatility for different applications. Lastly, Glass Wool refers to fiberglass insulation made by melting glass and converting it into fibers, resulting in a fluffy and lightweight material that is easy to install and offers excellent thermal insulation properties.</p></p>

<p><p>Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) Controllers refer to devices used to regulate the amount of power delivered to electronic devices. Current Mode PWM Controllers enable switching power supplies by analyzing the current through the power devices. They achieve stability and good dynamic response. On the other hand, Voltage Mode PWM Controllers monitor and regulate voltage feedback for power devices, ensuring stable power conversion. Both types serve to efficiently control power delivery and maintain stability, but they use different methods to regulate either the current or voltage.</p></p>

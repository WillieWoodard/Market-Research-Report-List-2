Organic finishing auxiliaries are derived from natural sources and are generally biodegradable. They are used in textile finishing processes, such as dyeing and printing, to improve colorfastness and provide a soft feel. In contrast, inorganic finishing auxiliaries are synthetic chemicals that offer enhanced performance and durability. They are commonly used in sectors like construction and automotive for applications like corrosion resistance and waterproofing.

<p><p>There are two types of driving simulation markets: compact simulators and full-scale simulators. Compact simulators are smaller in size and offer virtual driving experiences through screens or monitors. They provide a cost-effective solution for training and research purposes. On the other hand, full-scale simulators offer a more immersive experience by replicating a real vehicle cab with actual controls. They provide a realistic training environment for professional drivers and are widely used in the aviation and automotive industries.</p></p>

<p><p>A fluorescence spectrophotometer is a device used to measure the fluorescence emission of a sample when it is excited with a specific wavelength of light. There are different types of fluorescence spectrophotometers, including single beam type and double beam type. </p><p>A single beam type has a single light beam that passes through both the sample and the reference cell, while a double beam type splits the light beam into two paths, one passing through the sample and the other through a reference cell. The double beam type offers increased precision and accuracy due to its ability to compensate for variations in the light source or sample conditions.</p></p>

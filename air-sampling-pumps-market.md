<p><p>Air sampling pumps are devices used to collect air samples for various purposes such as environmental monitoring, occupational health, and indoor air quality assessments. These pumps are categorized into two types: high flow and low flow. </p><p>High flow pumps have the ability to collect larger volumes of air per minute, typically around 5 to 30 liters, making them suitable for sampling in open spaces or when a high concentration of contaminants is expected. </p><p>On the other hand, low flow pumps are designed to collect smaller volumes of air, typically less than 5 liters per minute. These pumps are used when sampling in confined spaces or when a longer sampling time is required to capture lower concentration contaminants accurately.</p></p>

<p><p>Gas chromatographic columns are used in gas chromatography, a technique used for separating and analyzing mixtures of gases or volatile substances. Packed columns are filled with a solid or porous material, which provides a large surface area for the sample to interact with, leading to efficient separation. Capillary columns, on the other hand, have a narrow inner diameter and are coated with a stationary phase. These columns offer high resolution and are suitable for analyzing complex mixtures. Both types of columns play a crucial role in gas chromatography, each offering distinct advantages in various applications.</p></p>

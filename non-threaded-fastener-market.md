<p><p>The non-threaded fastener market includes various types such as screws, nuts, rivets, and others. Screws are the most commonly used fasteners, employed to join two objects by rotating them into a pre-drilled hole. Nuts, on the other hand, are used with bolts to secure objects by creating a threaded connection. Rivets are permanent fasteners that require the application of force to join materials together. Other types of non-threaded fasteners can include pins, clips, and washers, each serving different purposes in joining components without the need for threading.</p></p>

<p><p>Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM) is a diagnostic process that involves measuring drug levels in a patient's blood to optimize the dosage and ensure effective treatment while minimizing potential side effects. It is primarily used for drugs with a narrow therapeutic index or where individual variation in drug metabolism is common. TDM tests help clinicians make informed decisions about adjusting drug dosage, ensuring therapeutic efficacy, and avoiding toxic effects.</p><p>The Therapeutic Drug Monitoring Test Market is experiencing significant growth and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 7.4% during the forecast period. These include immunoassays, chromatography, and mass spectrometry-based techniques that offer improved accuracy, precision, and sensitivity in drug level measurements. Furthermore, advancements in point-of-care testing are facilitating easier, faster, and cost-effective TDM, enabling real-time adjustments in drug dosages.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the increasing adoption of TDM tests in therapeutic areas such as psychiatry, cardiology, and oncology. TDM tests are being used to optimize the dosage of drugs used in the treatment of mental health disorders, cardiovascular diseases, and cancer, thereby improving patient outcomes and reducing healthcare costs.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has also impacted the Therapeutic Drug Monitoring Test Market.

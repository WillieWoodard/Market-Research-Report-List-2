<p><p>Chelate fertilizers are a type of plant nutrient fertilizers that are fortified with chelating agents to enhance the availability and uptake of micronutrients by plants. These agents form soluble complexes with the nutrients, preventing them from being tied up by soil particles and making them more accessible to plants. Chelate fertilizers are commonly used to supply micronutrients like iron, zinc, manganese, and copper to crops, which are essential for their growth and development.</p><p>The global chelate fertilizer market is witnessing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 11.7% during the forecast period. <p><p>The chelate fertilizer market can be categorized into three types based on nutrient composition. Primary nutrients include nitrogen, phosphorous, and potassium, which are essential for overall plant development. Secondary nutrients encompass calcium, magnesium, and sulfur, playing a crucial role in enzyme functions and growth regulation. Lastly, micronutrients refer to trace elements like iron, zinc, manganese, copper, boron, and molybdenum, which are required in small quantities for various biochemical processes in plants. These different types of chelate fertilizers offer specific nutrient combinations to cater to different plant needs.</p></p>

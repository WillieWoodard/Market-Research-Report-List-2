<p><p>An Impulse Hydro Turbine is a type of hydro turbine that is widely used for the generation of electricity from water resources. Unlike other types of hydro turbines, which generate power from both the kinetic energy and potential energy of water, impulse turbines solely utilize the kinetic energy of the water flow. These turbines are designed with a set of nozzles that direct high-pressure water onto the turbine blades, causing it to spin and generate mechanical energy. The mechanical energy is then converted into electrical energy by a generator.</p><p>The future outlook of the Impulse Hydro Turbine market is highly positive. <p><p>The impulse hydro turbine market consists of two main types: the Pelton hydro turbine and the cross flow hydro turbine. A Pelton hydro turbine is designed to operate in high head conditions, where water is directed onto a series of spoon-shaped buckets mounted around the perimeter of a wheel. This causes the wheel to rotate and generate electricity. On the other hand, a cross flow hydro turbine is suitable for low head conditions, where water flows horizontally through the turbine, rotating the runner and generating power. Both types have their specific applications based on head conditions.</p></p>

<p><p>Fuel ethanol is a type of biofuel produced from renewable sources like crops and plant residues. There are three main types of fuel ethanol markets: starch-based, sugar-based, and cellulosic. Starch-based fuel ethanol is derived from crops like corn and wheat, while sugar-based fuel ethanol is produced from sugarcane or sugar beets. Cellulosic fuel ethanol, on the other hand, is made from non-edible plant materials such as corn stover or wood chips. These different types of fuel ethanol offer alternative options for reducing dependence on fossil fuels, enhancing energy security, and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.</p></p>

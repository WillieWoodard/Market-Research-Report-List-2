<p><p>The sound insulation glass market offers various types of glass that are designed to reduce noise transmission. Hollow glass, also known as double-glazed glass, consists of two glass panes with an air-filled gap in between, providing insulation against sound. Vacuum glass, on the other hand, has a vacuum layer between the glass panes, making it highly effective in noise reduction. Laminated glass is composed of two or more glass layers with an interlayer, offering both sound insulation and safety. These types of glass cater to different requirements for soundproofing in various applications.</p></p>

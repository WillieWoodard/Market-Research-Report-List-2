<p><p>Offshore wind turbines are categorized into two main types: horizontal axis and vertical axis turbines. Horizontal axis turbines have blades that rotate around a horizontal axis and are primarily used in large-scale offshore wind farms. They are more efficient and commonly preferred due to their ability to capture wind from any direction. On the other hand, vertical axis turbines have blades that rotate around a vertical axis and are suitable for small-scale installations. They are less efficient but can function in winds coming from multiple directions, making them a suitable choice for diverse offshore locations.</p></p>
